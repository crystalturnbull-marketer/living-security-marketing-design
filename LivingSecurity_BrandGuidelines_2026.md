## Brand Story

Cybersecurity was built around systems and networks. But the modern enterprise runs on people — and now on AI agents working alongside them. That's where risk actually lives, and where most security programs go quiet: treating every user the same, drowning teams in noise, and offering no real path to changing the behavior that drives the breach.

Living Security saw it differently from the start. The workforce is an organization's most powerful — and most underused — line of defense. We built the company on the conviction that people, not technology alone, protect the enterprise — and that when you change behavior, you change outcomes. From that origin in immersive cybersecurity experiences and behavior-driven training, we pioneered the Human Risk Management category — and today we lead it.

The Living Security Platform is AI-native: it delivers risk visibility no other approach can match, guides teams with explainable intelligence, and autonomously remediates threats at scale. Powered by Livvy, our intelligence layer, we turn billions of behavioral signals into clear, confident action — helping organizations transform human risk into organizational resilience and build a workforce that actively defends what matters.

## The Voice

We communicate with clarity and confidence, turning complex human risk into actionable insight, guiding people forward with empathy, and focusing on measurable outcomes that shape the future of security.

### Brand Voice Attributes

#### 1. Intelligent *(not jargon-heavy)*

We understand complex security challenges. We explain clearly without dumbing down. We use precise language but avoid acronym soup.

> **Do:** "10% of users drive 73% of risk"
> **Don't:** "Statistically significant risk concentration in minority user cohorts"

#### 2. Confident *(not arrogant)*

We're category leaders and we own it. We make bold claims backed by data. We don't hedge or qualify unnecessarily.

> **Do:** "We created HRM. Now we're evolving it."
> **Don't:** "We believe we may have pioneered..."

#### 3. Empathetic *(not preachy)*

Security is about people, not just technology. We guide, never shame or blame. We acknowledge that everyone makes mistakes.

> **Do:** "78% of your workforce actively reduces risk — let's empower them."
> **Don't:** "Most employees fail security training."

#### 4. Actionable *(not theoretical)*

Every message should drive toward an outcome. Concrete examples over abstract concepts. Proof points, not promises.

> **Do:** "60% reduction in time users spend at risk"
> **Don't:** "Significantly improved security posture"

#### 5. Future-Focused *(not alarmist)*

We paint a vision without fear-mongering. Opportunity, not just threat. We're proactive, not reactive.

> **Do:** "Secure tomorrow's workforce today."
> **Don't:** "The threats are coming for you."

### The Grammar

**Writing Style — Do:**

- Use active voice ("We identify risks" not "Risks are identified")
- Lead with benefits, support with features
- Use concrete numbers and proof points
- Break up long paragraphs (3–4 sentences max for digital)
- Include specific examples and scenarios
- Ask questions that the audience is already thinking
- Use contractions for warmth (we're, don't, can't)

**Writing Style — Don't:**

- Use fear, uncertainty, or doubt (FUD) tactics
- Blame users for security failures
- Over-use security jargon or acronyms
- Make unsubstantiated claims
- Use all caps for emphasis (use bold sparingly instead)
- Write in passive voice
- Bury the lead — get to the point quickly

---

### The Industry Terms

**Use These Terms:**

| Use | Instead of |
|---|---|
| Workforce | Employees / Users (when possible) |
| Vigilant | Compliant |
| Empower | Enforce |
| Guide | Control |
| Autonomous | Automated |
| Intelligence | AI (when overused) |

**Avoid These Terms:**

- **Weak link** — dehumanizing
- **Enforce / Police** — authoritarian
- **Surveillance** — privacy concerns
- **Punish / Penalize** — negative framing
- **Dumb down** — condescending
- **Click here** — outdated, accessibility issue

### Clear Space

The logo's safe zone is equivalent to the height and width of the corner "squares" on all sides. This clearspace zone indicates the closest any other graphic element or message can be positioned in relation to all logo formats. They have a fixed relationship that should never be changed in any way.

### Logo Integrity

For the purpose of brand integrity and consistency, the Living Security logo should not be altered. Here are some examples of how not to treat our logos.

**Do Not:**

- Size the logo down beyond legibility
- Crop, compress, or stretch the logo
- Display the logo in colors or styles outside of what's outlined in this guide
- Rearrange any of the logo elements
- Add shadows, highlights, or any other effects
- Add any extra elements to the logo or mark

## The Aesthetic

### Textures and Backgrounds

Living Security's visual system includes three families of approved textures and backgrounds:

**Light / Neutral** — White wave forms, white fingerprint patterns, white contour lines. For clean, editorial layouts.

**Brand Dark** — Red and black wave forms, red fingerprint patterns, dark dot-matrix waves. For high-impact brand moments.

**Livvy / Product** — Cyan and teal dot-matrix waves, cyan particle fields, dark gradient fields. Use only in Livvy or product contexts.

### Photo & Video Direction

**The Visual Dos**

- Technology meets lifestyle
- Positive, collaborative, human
- Color overlays acceptable
- Inclusive of the many diverse people and environments that we operate in
- Authentic and unposed — scenes should look like a snapshot of real life
- Black and white imagery

**The Visual Don'ts**

- Hoodies/hackers
- Surveillance imagery, stressed or alarmed expressions
- Militaristic metaphors
- Fake stock situations
- Overly bright or washed-out looks
- Green or purple tones
